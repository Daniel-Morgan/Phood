Creating a recipe:

  1. If you find a recipe that you'd like to share with others, locate the nav bar. It should be in the upper position of your page. In that nav-bar click on the button named "Create Recipe"

  2. You will need to insert a recipe name that suits that of your dish. Please keep in mind that this page will be made public so restrain from any profane words or dishes. Once you have inserted a name, go ahead and add a description that gives users an idea what your recipe will be such as tastes, and what events its good for.

  3. Once you've created your description select the category for which your recipe falls into, then go step by step for the directions to cook your meal. For other people's conveniences be as thorough as possible with your directions

  4. Once you've created a recipe add in all of the ingredient that will be required for your dish. The ingredient are listed in 3 columns, quantity, measurements, and description of what that ingredient is.

Tips for creating a popular recipe:
  • Use capital letters in your recipe name
  • The description is your time to shine, describe clearly and effectively what makes your recipe great. Include why others might like it.
  • Do not use fractions. Phood uses decimals for quantity information since using many different measurement systems may confuse our users.
  • Units should be in title case format. Eg. "Cups".

Using the home page

The home page shows the top five recipes for each category based on our rating system. Each recipe is displayed in a card showing the name, description, creation date, and rating. You can click the "view" button to see the recipe in greater detail. There is a navbar at the top of the page that you can use to navigate to other pages like

  • Create Recipe
  • My Profile
  • Search

Viewing your recipes

When viewing your recipe, you will be presented with the recipe display page. At the top of the page, you will notice your recipe's title, its likes displayed next to a gold star, and the category that the recipe belongs to. On the right side of the page, you will find your username, your full name, and your top two recipes sorted by the most likes. Clicking on one of those recipes will take you to their associated display pages. The like and dislike buttons can be found on the left side of the page, and clicking one will add or subtract a like from the recipe, respectively. Underneath the title, number of likes, and the category, you will see a helpful description of the recipe. Below the description, the needed ingredients for the recipe are listed, displaying their quantity, units, and the name of the ingredient itself. Lastly, on the bottom of the page, the recipe preparation can be found, sufficiently explaining how to produce the desired recipe.
